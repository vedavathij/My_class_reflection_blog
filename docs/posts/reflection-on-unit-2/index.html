Exploring Answers To Curious Questions

In unit 2, we learned many new things which answers many little questions we may wonder sometimes.

1: Have you ever wondered how images form on your screen?

Yes, I have always wondered how it actually works. I remember looking into television very closely to see what is there inside tv which make visuals. In my home, we had two television, one is color TV and another one is black-and-white TV. May be that made me more curious ‘why both are different?’. During this unit I learned that screens are made of tiny little box which is called ‘pixels’. its often called as ‘units’ or ‘cells’ of digital picture. Each pixel displays color which make a whole picture. Intresting factor I learned is, in color digital picture, the pixels only has 3 colors ‘red’, ‘green’ and ‘blue’.by adjusting there values they form different colors. Which brings me back to the topic primary colors in art and how important this colors are. Not only this, we also learned about transformers, shifting, how image is blurred, compressed and what happens when it does.

2: Optimization and formal analysis

In this topic I encountered many simple questions yet found hard to answers. At first when you see the questions, it seems easy but while solving we need to think more carefully. There were 3 sub topics under this topic.

  • Greedy and Dynamic Programming - It thought me how to not blindly choose things just because of high values.
  • Train Graphs - this one is very interesting topic. Have you ever wondered how trains never collide eachother? or how trains system actually works?. ‘Crossings of train’ we hear in everyday life is the answer. There will be trains which waits to let another train pass through and the graphs teaches how it works.
  • Knapsack problem - when you go to buy something in market, did you ever got trouble in picking things when you have offers with fixed capacity bag?. In this topic we learned how to pick things which gives maximum value without exceeding given capacity (or maximum capacity).

3: Conversions

We learned how computer converts and understand the numbers we use. We know that, computer only know binary language but still it is able to read all the things we type. by this topic, we understood how a computer read the numbers as binary and how it works.

4 : Konigsberg bridge problem

This problem is really puzzled and need lot of focus.It is about finding a path that crosses all bridges exactly once without repeating any. At first, it seemed like a simple puzzle, but it actually led to the concept of graph theory.If you want to give brain exercise? this one is best.
